Safety First

Stay safe as we fall back

This Sunday marks the end of Daylight Saving Time — don’t forget to turn your clocks back one hour! While the extra sleep is nice, the time change can increase the risk of fatigue and accidents both on the road and at work.

As evening darkness arrives earlier, visibility decreases right around rush hour. Studies show a spike in traffic accidents and pedestrian injuries in the days following the switch. The change also disrupts our sleep schedules, leaving many people drowsier than usual.

Keep these safety tips in mind as we “fall back”:

  • Stay alert on the road. Watch for pedestrians and adjust to darker evening commutes.
  • Check smoke and CO alarms. Replace batteries and outdated units (smoke alarms older than 10 years, CO alarms older than 5).
  • Prepare your vehicle. Stock a winter emergency kit with warm clothing, snacks, a flashlight, and jumper cables.
  • Inspect fire extinguishers. Make sure the gauge is in the green zone.

A little preparation and awareness go a long way in keeping everyone safe this season.

Welcome to the Seegars Fence IT Workspace

We’ve rolled out something new to make IT communication smoother and faster. The Seegars Fence IT Workspace is your new one-stop spot for submitting IT tickets, checking updates, and browsing a growing knowledge base built from real support requests.

The goal is simple: to keep things organized and help you get solutions quicker without all the back-and-forth emails.
